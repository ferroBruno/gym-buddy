# Exercise Substitutions

## Use

Use when the user asks about replacing one exercise with another.

## Guidance

- A substitution can be reasonable when the goal, equipment and comfort are compatible.
- Similar exercises are not identical.
- Ask or infer the reason for the swap: equipment, preference, pain, difficulty, or time.
- If the reason is equipment or preference, suggest a practical alternative.
- If the reason is pain, apply safety boundaries and avoid treating it as a normal substitution.

## Boundaries

- Do not claim two exercises are perfectly equivalent.
- Do not recommend training through pain.
- Do not diagnose why an exercise hurts.
