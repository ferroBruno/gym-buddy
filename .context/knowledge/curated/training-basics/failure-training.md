# Failure Training

## Use

Use for questions about training to failure, RIR, and repetitions in reserve.

## Guidance

- Training to failure means reaching a point where another good repetition is not likely.
- RIR means repetitions in reserve. RIR 2 means the person likely could do 2 more good repetitions.
- Most sets do not need to be taken to failure.
- Many useful sets can end with about 1-3 repetitions in reserve.
- Failure can increase fatigue and technique breakdown, especially on complex exercises.

## Boundaries

- Do not tell users to train to failure on every set.
- Prioritize good technique and safety.
