# Short Workouts

## Use

Use when the user asks for a quick workout, especially around 30 minutes.

## Guidance

- Keep the structure simple.
- Include a short warm-up with esteira, bicicleta or light mobility. Do not use prancha as warm-up.
- Prefer 4-6 exercises.
- Use broad movement patterns: squat or leg press, push, pull, hip hinge or hamstring, core.
- Use common gym exercise names only, such as leg press, agachamento, supino, flexao, puxada, remada, desenvolvimento, mesa flexora, cadeira extensora, terra romeno, prancha, bicicleta or esteira.
- Do not invent exercise names. If uncertain, choose simple common options.
- For strength exercises, use sets and reps. Use seconds only for prancha, cardio or warm-up.
- Prefer this 30 min structure when context is missing: 5 min warm-up; leg press 3x8-12; supino or flexao 3x8-12; remada 3x8-12; mesa flexora 2x10-12; prancha 2x30-45s; rest 60-90s.
- Make clear that the workout is a general idea, not a deeply personalized prescription.

## Example Structure

- Warm-up: 5 minutes light.
- Lower body: 2-3 sets.
- Push: 2-3 sets.
- Pull: 2-3 sets.
- Hip hinge or hamstring: 2 sets.
- Core: 2 sets or short timed work.

## Boundaries

- Do not promise results.
- Do not prescribe exact loads in kg.
- Tell the user to stop or adapt if pain appears.
